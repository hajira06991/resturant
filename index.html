Our Philosophy

Craft Without
Compromise

L'Étoile Dorée was born from a single conviction: that French cuisine, at its most honest, needs no spectacle. Only intention. Our kitchen has never chased trends — it has quietly set them.

"The table is where memory is made. We are merely the architects of the moment."

Every plate that leaves our kitchen carries the weight of decades — of relationships with farmers in Brittany, truffle hunters in Périgord, and fishermen on the Normandy coast.

The Mind Behind the Menu

Chef Julien
Moreau

Executive Chef & Co-Proprietor

Julien Moreau spent his formative years between the markets of Lyon and the kitchens of Alain Ducasse, learning that restraint is the most difficult technique to master. He returned to Paris at thirty-two, took the helm of L'Étoile Dorée, and within four years earned its second Michelin star.

His cooking is personal — shaped by a childhood in coastal Brittany, an obsession with heritage seed varieties, and a belief that the finest ingredient is always the one coaxed from a relationship built over years, not transactions.

Our Story

Three Decades
of Quiet Excellence

L'Étoile Dorée opened in 1987, a small room on Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré with fourteen covers and an unwavering commitment to market-led French cuisine. The founders, Bernard and Céleste Moreau, believed that a restaurant should feel like a home — one where the cooking is extraordinary and the welcome is genuine.

"We didn't open a restaurant. We opened a room where people could feel what food is capable of."
— Bernard Moreau, Founder

In 2012, their son Julien assumed the role of Executive Chef, earning the restaurant its second Michelin star the following year. The philosophy has never changed. The ambition has only deepened.
